Hemodynamic sensors are devices that detect any change in the hemodynamic performance of the heart. Any variation in the hemodynamic performance of the heart is primarily due to the autonomic nervous system-induced regulation of the myocardial fibers. Chronotropic and inotropic properties are controlled, under normal physiological conditions, to modulate the cardiac output of a person according to his metabolic demand. When the hemodynamic compensation is achieved according to the requirement of the body, the system is negatively regulated. Different types of hemodynamic sensors are being designed these days to monitor different phases of cardiac contraction strength. As hemodynamic sensors are used in cardiac disorders, increase in the number of cardiovascular diseases will drive the market for hemodynamic sensors.
